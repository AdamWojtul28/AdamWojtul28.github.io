Metal Releases Notification Bot

Since I am a huge fan of metal music and I constantly am interested in finding new bands from my favorite subgenres, I decided to make several scripts that webscrape information from metal release calendars and lastfm.com to send me updates via a Telegram bot whenever a new album from one of my favorite metal subgenres is added to a metal release calendar. Currently, the webscraping functionality is complete and I am able to send myself updates via the Telegram bot manually using the Telegram API, but I now need to figure out how to schedule this process for a) new album release announcements (whenever an album is added to a calendar) and b) on release day, so I am aware of all albums that were released on a release day.

Teacher Assistant Application Redesign

As part of my Human Computer Interaction course at the University of Florida (CAP5100), students were tasked to team up and improve upon an existing interface using the methodologies we had learned in our course. Since Sean, my teammate is a TA who had frustrations about the current Teacher Assistant Application System (TAAS), we decided to redesign the system. We started by conducting research and seeing what problems other users had with the existing system and we brainstormed ways to improve the system. I then made a paper prototype of the system (a total of around 20 screens) that Sean made a Figma based off of. After this process was completed, Sean and I got to work on the application itself. Sean worked on the login, student, and professor sides of the application while I worked exclusively on the System-Administrator side of the app, since it had the most functionality. As part of the system-admin side, I worked on creating a dashboard that showed action items in the form of both courses and students that needed assignment and the respective home pages for each of those to see the remainder of the items. I also worked on all reports and documentation related to the project, contributing to the Related Works, User Requirements, Interface Design, and Discussion and recording the video demo of our project.

Senior Project - TablerEnabler Application

As part of my Senior Project course at the University of Florida (CIS4914), students were tasked to create groups and come up with projects that showcased what they had learned during their time at the University of Florida. Since a lot of my non-core coursework related to creating user-friendly designs, our group decided to research about a problem that existed on our campus that we could then go out and solve. We noticed the problem that lots of student organizations table without much success on campus and therefore we wanted to create an application that did a better job at connecting students to student organizations who are tabling, which we called TablerEnabler. We started off by conducting end-user research, where we interviewed students and student organizations about our application, trying to understand their problems. We then created paper prototypes and a Figma prototype to get feedback on the interface that we had in mind to address this problem. Upon receiving positive feedback, our group took to developing; in this group I had the role of developing the backend using MongoDB. I first developed an Entity-Relationship Diagram and a Relational Schema for how tables relate to one another. I made all of these tables in MongoDB and I am now in the process of developing RESTful API endpoints to allow the frontend to extract relevant information from the database. Once I set up the database tables, my focus turned to helping my teammates develop the frontend side of the application. I was responsible for fully developing the organization profile, favorites, (future) map-view and system-administrator practically in their totality and I helped touch up the search page and the buttons on each page so that they navigated to the corresponding page of interest. I was heavily involved in this project and along with Mikhail, I was one of the two project managers for this project. I am proud of how it turned out.

BitTorrent Clone - Computer Network Fundamentals Semester Project

In my Computer Network Fundamentals class at the University of Florida, we were told by our professor to divide ourselves into groups of 4 to develop a BitTorrent clone using Java that allows peers to connect over a peer-to-peer network architecture. As part of this group, I worked in an AGILE environment, meeting weekly with my group to report progress and create new tasks. As part of this group, I developed the preliminary (later updated) architecture to connect peers with one another using TCP connections and created the handshaking response function, implemented functionality that would allow a peer to obtain file pieces of interest from preferred neighbors and to send relevant pieces to its preferred neighbors, and I finalized the packaging for the project, ensuring that it would work on non-local computers.

Product Related Injuries App - Information and Database Systems 1 Semester Project

In my Information and Database Systems 1 class, the class was tasked to divide itself into groups of about 4 students to work on a semester-long project. Since the scope of the project was open, so long as the dataset we used had over 200,000 datapoints and our project incorporated at least 6 complex SQL queries, our group decided to make an information app to alert people about products that are responsible for a lot of injuries for individuals; the app would also show what age groups are most affected by certain products. In my group, I developed the backend for the web application using Golang and SQL and designed a RESTful backend server that provides information about the products in an Oracle database when called by the frontend. I was also responsible for the development of all of the complex queries; some of these queries included finding what products were in the top 10 deadliest product every month over the course of the dataset timeframe, finding which products were most dangerous in the summer months, and which products had the longest consecutive days resulting in an individual's injuries. During the day of the presentation, I demoed the functionality of the backend and explained all of the queries to my professor.

Student Organization Hub UX Design Project

Our semester project in UX Design was to research what kinds of students were having on our campus and subsequently design the interface for an application that would solve one of the major problems that was mentioned. After completing initial interviews, our group determined that many students mentioned that it was quite difficult to easily find information about student organizations that would suit them. We also interviewed several officers from student organizations on our campus and we learned that many student organizations have to rely on using several apps to accommodate all of their needs. So, we designed an app that served two purposes: 1) to be a hub for all student organizations to promote their club, with features that would allow students to more easily find their club and 2) once those users join, to serve as the platform that would contain everything the club needed to operate, with features like group chats, announcements, and a calendar. Our group went through a significant portion of the UX Design process, from gathering information about the problems to developing a high fidelity prototype of the application; interviews were completed after every step of the process to receive feedback from potential users. After our group had a clear understanding of the students' needs, a low fidelity prototype was made with paper models before subsequently transitioning to the final project, a high-fidelity prototype which was made using Figma.
